Masala Dosa

Masala Dosa is a popular South Indian crispy fermented rice pancake stuffed with a spicy and flavorful potato filling. It‚Äôs often served with coconut chutney and sambar, making for a delicious and hearty meal. ü•îüå∂üçΩ

Ingredients

  • Dosa Batter: 2 cups rice, 1 cup urad dal (split black gram), 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds, water (for soaking and grinding), salt to taste
  • Potato Filling:
    • 4 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
    • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
    • 2 green chilies, chopped
    • 1 tsp mustard seeds
    • 1 tsp chana dal (split chickpeas)
    • 1 tsp urad dal
    • 10-12 curry leaves
    • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
    • 2 tbsp oil
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped

Preparation Steps

  1. Prepare Dosa Batter: Soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately for 4-6 hours. Grind to a smooth batter adding water as needed. Mix well and ferment overnight (8-12 hours) at room temperature.
  2. Make Potato Filling: Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds. When they splutter, add chana dal, urad dal, curry leaves, and green chilies. Saut√© for a minute.
  3. Add Onions & Spices: Add sliced onions and turmeric powder, saut√© until onions turn translucent.
  4. Add Potatoes: Add mashed potatoes, salt, and mix well. Cook for 2-3 minutes. Garnish with chopped coriander and set aside.
  5. Cook Dosa: Heat a non-stick pan or griddle. Pour a ladleful of fermented batter and spread it thin in a circular motion. Drizzle some oil around edges and cook until the dosa is crisp and golden brown.
  6. Assemble: Place a spoonful of potato filling on one side of the dosa and fold it over.
  7. Serve: Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
