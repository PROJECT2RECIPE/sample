Sambar

Ingredients

  • 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, drumstick, pumpkin, beans, etc.), chopped
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • 1 small lemon-sized tamarind ball (soaked in warm water)
  • 2-3 dried red chilies
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 2 tsp sambar powder
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 10-12 curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Preparation Steps

  1. Cook Dal: Rinse toor dal well and pressure cook with turmeric and enough water until soft and mushy. Mash slightly and set aside.
  2. Prepare Tamarind Water: Soak tamarind in warm water for 15 minutes, squeeze and strain to extract tamarind juice. Discard solids.
  3. Cook Vegetables: In a pot, add chopped vegetables, tomatoes, tamarind water, salt, and sambar powder. Add water as needed and cook until vegetables are tender.
  4. Combine Dal & Veggies: Add the cooked dal to the vegetable mixture. Adjust consistency by adding water if required. Bring to a gentle boil and simmer for 5 minutes.
  5. Prepare Tempering: Heat oil or ghee in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add fenugreek seeds, dried red chilies, curry leaves, and a pinch of asafoetida. Saut√© briefly.
  6. Add Tempering to Sambar: Pour the tempering over the sambar and mix well.
  7. Garnish & Serve: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with steamed rice, idli, or dosa.
